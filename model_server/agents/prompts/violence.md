# Violence Detection Guidelines

This ruleset operates under the `VIOLENCE` scenario. Follow these policies before raising a tier-2 alert.

## Detection Rules
- **Rule 1**: Physical contact with aggressive intent (hitting, pushing, kicking, grabbing) must be visible.
- **Rule 2**: Two or more persons in close confrontation with raised fists or aggressive postures.
- **Rule 3**: A person being restrained, cornered, or forcefully held against their will.
- **Rule 4**: Thrown objects aimed at another person.
- **Rule 5**: Visible weapons (knife, bat, improvised weapons) being brandished or used.

## Confirmation Signals
- **Signal A**: Sudden rapid movements with body contact between individuals.
- **Signal B**: One person falling or being knocked to the ground by another.
- **Signal C**: Bystanders reacting with alarm, running away, or calling for help.
- **Signal D**: Destruction of property (smashing, breaking) during the confrontation.

## Exclusion Rules
- **Rule 6**: Friendly physical contact (handshakes, hugs, pats on the back) should NOT trigger VIOLENCE.
- **Rule 7**: Sports activities or martial arts practice in appropriate settings should NOT trigger VIOLENCE.
- **Rule 8**: Children playing roughly (tag, wrestling on playground) should NOT trigger VIOLENCE unless injury-risk is apparent.
- **Rule 9**: Crowded movement or accidental bumping in dense areas should NOT trigger VIOLENCE.
- **Rule 10**: Security personnel performing authorized restraint should be flagged as RESTRAINT, not VIOLENCE.

## Severity Escalation
- Verbal confrontation with aggressive posture → LOW priority (monitor)
- Physical contact / shoving → MEDIUM priority (Tier-2 validation)
- Active assault with potential injury → HIGH priority (immediate alert + clip save)
- Weapon visible → CRITICAL (immediate alert + all clips saved)
